<p><strong>Company Summary</strong></p><p><strong>Tesla, Inc.</strong> is an American electric vehicle and clean energy company. It designs, develops, manufactures, sells, and leases high-performance, fully electric vehicles, solar energy systems, and related products and services. The company currently manufactures the Model 3, Model Y, Model S, Model X, and Tesla Semi vehicles. It also manufactures and sells energy storage products, such as the Powerwall and Megapack, and solar energy systems, such as Solar Roof and Solar Panels.</p><p>Tesla's mission is to accelerate the world's transition to sustainable energy. The company believes that electric vehicles, solar energy, and other clean energy technologies are critical to reducing greenhouse gas emissions and combating climate change. Tesla is committed to investing in and developing innovative products and technologies that will help to make the world a more sustainable place.</p><p><strong>Business Segments</strong></p><p>Tesla operates two reportable segments:</p><ul><li><strong>Automotive:</strong> This segment includes the design, development, manufacturing, sales, and leasing of high-performance, fully electric vehicles, as well as sales of automotive regulatory credits.</li><li><strong>Energy Generation and Storage:</strong> This segment includes the design, manufacture, installation, sales, and leasing of solar energy generation and energy storage products and related services.</li></ul><p><strong>Key Products and Services</strong></p><p><strong>Automotive</strong></p><ul><li><strong>Model 3:</strong> A Storage</strong></p><ul><li><strong>Powerwall:</strong> A lithium-ion battery that stores energy from solar panels or the grid for use during power outages or to reduce electricity costs.</li><li><strong>Megapack:</strong> A large-scale, lithium-ion battery that is used for commercial and industrial applications.</li><li><strong>Solar Roof:</strong> A roof system that surface.</li></ul><p><strong>Technology</strong></p><ul><li><strong>Electric Powertrain:</strong> Tesla's vehicles are powered by electric motors that are designed and manufactured in-house. The company's electric powertrains are known for their high efficiency and performance.</li><li><strong>Battery Technology:</strong> Tesla's vehicles use lithium-ion batteries that are designed and manufactured in-house. The company's batteries are known for their long range and durability.</li><li><strong>Vehicle Control Software:</strong> Tesla's vehicles are controlled by sophisticated software that is developed in-house. This software includes the operating system, infotainment system, and driver assistance systems.</li><li><strong>Autopilot and Full Self-Driving (FSD):</strong> Tesla's vehicles are equipped with a suite of advanced driver assistance systems that are designed to make driving safer and more convenient. FSD is a more advanced system that is designed to allow for fully autonomous driving.</li></ul><p><strong>Sales and Marketing</strong></p><p>Tesla sells its vehicles and energy products directly to customers through its website, showrooms, and service centers. The company also has a network of authorized resellers that sell and service its products. Tesla's marketing strategy is focused on creating awareness of its products and generating demand through word-of-mouth, social media, and online advertising.</p><p><strong>Service and Warranty</strong></p><p>Tesla provides service and warranty for its vehicles and energy products through its network of company-owned service centers and mobile service technicians. The company also offers extended warranties and service plans for its products.</p><p><strong>Financial Services</strong></p><p>Tesla offers a variety of financial services to its customers, including vehicle financing, leasing, and insurance. The company uses advanced manufacturing techniques and automation to reduce costs and improve quality.</p><p><strong>Supply Chain</strong></p><p>Tesla's supply chain is global, with components sourced from suppliers around the world.
